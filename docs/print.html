Documentation for LieGT

Overview

This Python package is made the following purposes:

  • Computes the root data for classical Lie algebra.
  • Computes the Gelfand-Tsetlin basis for finite dimensional representations.
  • Computing the matrix elements for finite dimensional representations.

This package requires sympy.

The GitHub repo tyotakuki/LieGT

Type An

Creates the root data object for \(A_n\).

The rank of the root system.

The list of roots of \(A_n\) in the usual basis \(e_i - e_j\) for \(i\neq j\). Roots are stored as sympy.Matrix objects.

an.RootDatAn.simple_roots

The list of simple roots of \(A_n\) in the usual basis \(e_i - e_{i+1}\) for \(1\leq i \leq n\).

an.RootDatAn.simple_coroots

Returns the list of simple coroots in the usual basis.

an.RootDatAn.cartan_matrix

Returns the Cartan matrix.

an.RootDatAn.fundamental_weights

Returns the list of fundamental weights. Fundamental weights are stored as sympy.Matrix objects.

an.RootDatAn.root_strings

Returns the list of roots in the fundamental weight basis. They are stored as lists with integer entries.

an.RootDatAn.positive_root_strings

Returns the list of positive roots in the fundamental weight basis. They are stored as lists with integer entries.

an.RootDatAn.highest_root_string

Returns highest root in the fundamental weight basis.

an.RootDatAn.highest_root

Returns the highest root in the usual basis.

an.RootDatAn.root_order

Returns the dictionary of a lexiographic order of roots. Roots are stored as tuples.

an.RootDatAn.positive_root_order

Returns the dictionary of a lexiographic order of positive roots. Roots are stored as tuples.

an.RootDatAn.get_p(root_string_alpha, root_string_beta)

Returns the greatest positive integer \(p_{\alpha,\beta}\) such that \(\beta-p\alpha\) is a root.

Returns a choice of \(\epsilon_{\alpha,\beta}=\pm 1\) in the commutation relation \([E_\alpha, E_\beta]=\epsilon_{\alpha,\beta}(p_{\alpha,\beta}+1)E_{\alpha+\beta}\) of the Chevalley basis.

Gelfand-Tsetlin Basis of Type An

A Gelfand-Tsetlin pattern for type \(A_{n-1}\) is a pyramid of (nonnegative) integers \[\begin{array}{ccccccc} \lambda_{n,1} & & \lambda_{n,2} & & \ldots & & \lambda_{n,n}\\ & \lambda_{n-1,1} & & \ldots & & \lambda_{n-1,n-1}\\ & & \ldots & & \ldots\\ & & \lambda_{2,1} & & \lambda_{2,2}\\ & & & \lambda_{1.1} \end{array}\] that satisfies the interlacing relation \[\lambda_{k,i} \geq \lambda_{k-1,i} \geq \lambda_{k,i+1}.\] The Gelfand-Tsetlin pattern records the branching of an irreducible representation with highest weight (as a partition)\[\lambda =(\lambda_{n,1}, \lambda_{n,2} ,\ldots,\lambda_{n,n} )\] along the chain of subgroups \[GL(n)\supset GL(n-1)\supset\ldots\supset GL(1).\]

class an.GTPattern(GT_pattern_as_list)

A Gelfand-Tsetlin pattern is usually stored as a list of rows. We can also use the class GTPattern to create a Gelfand-Tsetlin pattern object for convenience. The pattern is stored in the attribute an.GTPattern.pattern as a list. For example:

> b = GTPattern([[3, 2, 1], [2, 1], [1]])
> print(b.pattern)

[[3, 2, 1], [2, 1], [1]]

an.GTPattern.get_lambda(k, i)

Returns the entry \(\lambda_{k,i}\) for each pattern object.

an.GTPattern.get_l(k, i)

Returns the value of \(l_{k,i} = \lambda_{k,i} - i +1\) for each pattern object.

an.GTPattern.raise_pattern(k, i)
an.GTPattern.lower_pattern(k, i)

Raise/lower the entry \(\lambda_{k,i}\) pattern object by 1, respectively. Caution: These two methods do not have return values and will modify the original instance of GTPattern.

an.GTPattern.raised(k, i)
an.GTPattern.lowered(k, i)

Return new GTPattern objects with the entry \(\lambda_{k,i}\) raised/lowered by 1, respectively.

Caution: The methods provided in this class do not check the interlacing condition. The user will have to provide their own method for that purpose.

Matrix Element Formulas

The rational matrix element formulas are based on the formula given in:

  • Gelfand, Israel M., and Michael L. Tsetlin. Finite-dimensional representations of the group of unimodular matrices. Dokl. Akad. Nauk SSSR. Vol. 71. No. 8. 1950.
  • Molev, Alexander I. "Gelfand-Tsetlin bases for classical Lie algebras." arXiv preprint math/0211289 (2002).

Let \(E_{i,j}\) be the element in \(GL(n,\mathbb{C})\) with the \((i,j)\)-th entry equal to 1 and the rest equal to 0, the action of the generators of \(GL(n,\mathbb{C})\) on the Gelfand-Tsetlin basis elements \(v_\Lambda\) is given by: \[ E_{k,k}v_{\Lambda}=\left(\sum_{i=1}^{k}\lambda_{k,i}-\sum_{i=1}^{k-1}\lambda_{k-1,i}\right)v_{\Lambda} \] \[ E_{k,k+1}v_{\Lambda}=-\sum_{i=1}^{k}\frac{\prod_{r=1}^{k+1}\left(l_{k,i}-l_{k+1,r}\right)}{\prod_{r=1,r\neq i}^{k}\left(l_{k,i}-l_{k,r}\right)}v_{\Lambda_{k,i}^{+}} \] \[ E_{k+1,k}v_{\Lambda}=\sum_{i=1}^{k}\frac{\prod_{r=1}^{k-1}\left(l_{k,i}-l_{k-1,r}\right)}{\prod_{r=1,r\neq i}^{k}\left(l_{k,i}-l_{k,r}\right)}v_{\Lambda_{k,i}^{-}} \] It is possible to normalize the basis elements such that the restriction of the action to \(U(n)\subset GL(n,\mathbb{C})\) is unitary. The actions are thus given by: \[ E_{k,k+1}w_{\Lambda}=i\sum_{i=1}^{k}\sqrt{\frac{\prod_{r=1}^{k+1}\left(l_{k+1,r}-l_{k,i}\right)\prod_{r=1}^{k-1}\left(l_{k-1,r}-l_{k,i}-1\right)}{\prod_{r=1,r\neq i}^{k}\left(l_{k,r}-l_{k,i}\right)\left(l_{k,r}-l_{k,i}-1\right)}}w_{\Lambda_{k,i}^{+}} \] \[ E_{k+1,k}w_{\Lambda}=i\sum_{i=1}^{k}\sqrt{\frac{\prod_{r=1}^{k+1}\left(l_{k+1,r}-l_{k,i}+1\right)\prod_{r=1}^{k-1}\left(l_{k-1,r}-l_{k,i}\right)}{\prod_{r=1,r\neq i}^{k}\left(l_{k,r}-l_{k,i}+1\right)\left(l_{k,r}-l_{k,i}\right)}}w_{\Lambda_{k,i}^{+}} \]

Gelfand-Tsetlin Basis of Representations

A Gelfand-Tsetlin pattern of type \(C_n\) is a pyramid of nonnegative integers \[ \begin{array}{cccccccccc} \lambda_{n,1} & & \lambda_{n,2} & & \lambda_{n,3} & & \ldots & & \lambda_{n,n}\\ & \lambda_{n,1}' & & \lambda_{n,2}' & & \lambda_{n,3}' & & \ldots & & \lambda_{n,n}'\\ & & \lambda_{n-1,1} & & \lambda_{n-1,2} & & & & \lambda_{n-1,n-1}\\ & & & \lambda_{n-1.1}' & & \lambda_{n-1,2}' & & \ldots & & \lambda_{n-1.n-1}'\\ & & & & & \ldots & & & \ldots\\ & & & & & & & & \lambda_{1,1}\\ & & & & & & & & & \lambda_{1,1}' \end{array} \] satisfying the interlacing condition

\[ \lambda_{k,1} \geq\lambda_{k,1}^{\prime} \geq\lambda_{k,2} \geq\lambda_{k,2}^{\prime} \geq\ldots \geq\lambda_{k,k} \geq\lambda_{k,k}^{\prime} \geq 0 \] and \[ \lambda_{k,1}^{\prime} \geq\lambda_{k-1,1} \geq\lambda_{k,2}^{\prime} \geq\lambda_{k-1,2} \geq\ldots \geq\lambda_{k-1,k-1} \geq\lambda_{k,k}^{\prime} \geq 0 \] In

  • Molev, A. I. "A basis for representations of symplectic Lie algebras." Communications in mathematical physics 201 (1999): 591-618

for the sake of convenience, a Gelfand-Tsetlin pattern of \(C_n\) are modified to be the following pyramid of nonpositive integers: \[ \begin{array}{cccccccccc} & \lambda_{n,1} & & \lambda_{n,2} & & \lambda_{n,3} & & \ldots & & \lambda_{n,n}\\ \lambda_{n,1}' & & \lambda_{n,2}' & & \lambda_{n,3}' & & \ldots & & \lambda_{n,n}'\\ & \lambda_{n-1,1} & & \lambda_{n-1,2} & & & & \lambda_{n-1,n-1}\\ \lambda_{n-1.1}' & & \lambda_{n-1,2}' & & \ldots & & \lambda_{n-1.n-1}'\\ & & \ldots & & & \ldots\\ & \lambda_{1,1}\\ \lambda_{1,1}' \end{array} \] and the corresponding interlacing relation is \[ 0 \geq \lambda_{k,1} \geq\lambda_{k,1}^{\prime} \geq\lambda_{k,2} \geq\lambda_{k,2}^{\prime} \geq\ldots \geq\lambda_{k,k} \geq\lambda_{k,k}^{\prime} \] and \[ 0 \geq\lambda_{k,1}^{\prime} \geq\lambda_{k-1,1} \geq\lambda_{k,2}^{\prime} \geq\lambda_{k-1,2} \geq\ldots \geq\lambda_{k-1,k-1} \geq\lambda_{k,k}^{\prime} \] instead. In the class class GTPattern(GT_pattern_as_list), the methods are presumed to be handling the Molev convention of the Gelfand-Tsetlin patterns. The rows represented by \(\lambda_{k,l}^{\prime}\) are called auxillary rows and they represent the multiplicity space of the branching along the chain of subgroups \[ Sp(2n,\mathbb{C}) \supset Sp(2n-2,\mathbb{C}) \ldots Sp(2,\mathbb{C}) \] The following methods, similar to their counterparts for the \[A_n\] case, are provided to handle the raising and lowering operation on Gelfand-Tsetlin patterns. 

cn.GTPattern.raise_pattern(k,i)
cn.GTPattern.lower_pattern(k,i)
cn.GTPattern.raised(k,i)
cn.GTPattern.lowered(k,i)
cn.GTPattern.raise_aux_pattern(k,i)
cn.GTPattern.lower_aux_pattern(k,i)
cn.GTPattern.raised_aux(k,i)
cn.GTPattern.lowered_aux(k,i)
cn.GTPattern.get_lambda(k,i)
cn.GTPattern.get_lambda_aux(k,i)
cn.GTPattern.get_l(k,i)
cn.GTPattern.get_l_aux(k,i)

In the \(C_n\) case, \(l_{k,i}\) is given by \(l_{k,i} = \lambda_{k,i} - i\).

Matrix Element Formulas

The formula provided in Theorem 1.1 of

  • Molev, A. I. "A basis for representations of symplectic Lie algebras." Communications in mathematical physics 201 (1999): 591-618

gave the actions of the generators \(F_{i,j} = E_{i,j} - \left(\mathrm{sgn}i\cdot \mathrm{sgn}j\right) E_{-j,-i}\). The matrices are indexed by \(-n, -n+1, \ldots, -1,1,\ldots,n\).

\[ F_{kk}\zeta_{\Lambda}=\left(2\sum_{i=1}^{k}\lambda_{ki}^{\prime}-\sum_{i=1}^{k}\lambda_{ki}-\sum_{i=1}^{k-1}\lambda_{k-1,i}\right)\zeta_{\Lambda} \] \[ F_{k,-k}\zeta_{\Lambda}=\sum_{i=1}^{k}A_{ki}(\Lambda)\zeta_{\Lambda+\delta_{ki}^{\prime}} \] \[ F_{-k,k}\zeta_{\Lambda}=\sum_{i=1}^{k}B_{ki}(\Lambda)\zeta_{\Lambda-\delta_{ki}^{\prime}} \] \[ F_{k-1,-k}\zeta_{\Lambda}=\sum_{i=1}^{k-1}C_{ki}(\Lambda)\zeta_{\Lambda-\delta_{k-1,i}}+\sum_{i=1}^{k}\sum_{j,m=1}^{k-1}D_{kijm}(\Lambda)\zeta_{\Lambda+\delta_{ki}^{\prime}+\delta_{k-1,j}+\delta_{k-1,m}^{\prime}} \] with the coefficients \[ A_{ki}(\Lambda)=\prod_{a=1,a\neq i}^{k}\frac{1}{l_{ka}^{\prime}-l_{ki}^{\prime}} \] \[ B_{ki}(\Lambda)=4A_{ki}(\Lambda)l_{ki}^{\prime}\prod_{a=1}^{k}\left(l_{ka}-l_{ki}^{\prime}\right)\prod_{a=1}^{k-1}\left(l_{k-1,a}-l_{k,i}^{\prime}\right) \] \[ C_{ki}(\Lambda)=\frac{1}{2l_{k-1,i}}\prod_{a=1,a\neq i}^{k-1}\frac{1}{l_{k-1,i}^{2}-l_{k-1,a}^{2}} \] \[ \begin{array}{ll} D_{kijm}(\Lambda)& =A_{ki}(\Lambda)A_{k-1,m}(\Lambda)C_{kj}(\Lambda)\times \\ &\prod_{a=1,a\neq i}^{k}\left(l_{k-1,j}-l_{ka}^{\prime}\right)\left(l_{k-1,j}+l_{ka}^{\prime}+1\right)\prod_{a=1,a\neq m}^{k-1}\left(l_{k-1,j}-l_{k-1,a}^{\prime}\right)\left(l_{k-1,j}+l_{k-1,a}^{\prime}+1\right) \end{array} \]

Gelfand-Tsetlin Basis of Bn and Dn Representations

Gelfand-Tsetlin Patterns for Type Bn

A Gelfand-Tsetlin pattern for type \(B_n\) is a pyramid of either all nonnegative integers or all half-integers \[ \begin{array}{cccccccc} \lambda_{n,1} & & \lambda_{n,2} & & \ldots & & \lambda_{n,n}\\ & \lambda_{n,1}^\prime & & \lambda_{n,2}^\prime & & \ldots & & \lambda_{n,n}^\prime\\ & & \lambda_{n-1,1} & & \ldots & & \lambda_{n-1,n-1}\\ & & & \lambda_{n-1,1}^\prime & & \ldots & & \lambda_{n-1,n-1}^\prime\\ & & & & & & \ldots\\ & & & & & & \lambda_{1,1}\\ & & & & & & & \lambda_{1,1}^\prime \end{array} \] satisfying the interlacing conditions \[ \lambda_{k,1}\geq \lambda_{k,1}^\prime \geq \lambda_{k,2}\geq \lambda_{k,2}^\prime \geq \ldots\geq \lambda_{k,k-1}^\prime\geq \lambda_{k,k} \geq \left|\lambda_{k,k}^\prime\right| \] and \[ \lambda_{k,1}^\prime\geq \lambda_{k-1,1} \geq \lambda_{k,2}^\prime\geq \lambda_{k-1,2} \geq \ldots\geq \lambda_{k,k-1}^\prime\geq \lambda_{k-1,k-1} \geq \left|\lambda_{k,k}^\prime\right|. \] In Molev, Gelfand-Tsetlin patterns with negative entries and rows in decreasing order was used. However, we will not be following their convention.

Gelfand-Tsetlin Patterns for Type Dn

A Gelfand-Tsetlin pattern for type \(D_n\) is a pyramid of all nonnegative integers or all half-integers \[ \begin{array}{ccccccc} \lambda_{n,1} & & \lambda_{n,2} & & \ldots & & \lambda_{n,n}\\ & \lambda_{n-1,1}^\prime & & \ldots & & \lambda_{n-1,n-1}^\prime\\ & & \lambda_{n-1,1} & & \ldots & & \lambda_{n-1,n-1}\\ & & & & & \ldots\\ & & & & & \lambda_{1,1}^\prime\\ & & & & & & \lambda_{1,1} \end{array} \] satisfying the interlacing conditions \[ \lambda_{k,1}\geq \lambda_{k-1,1}^\prime \geq \lambda_{k,2}\geq \lambda_{k-1,2}^\prime \geq \ldots\geq \lambda_{k,k-1}\geq \lambda_{k-1,k-1}^\prime \geq \left|\lambda_{k,k}\right| \] and \[ \lambda_{k,1}^\prime\geq \lambda_{k,1} \geq \lambda_{k,2}^\prime\geq \lambda_{k,2} \geq \ldots\geq \lambda_{k,k-1}\geq \lambda_{k,k}^\prime \geq \left|\lambda_{k,k}\right|. \] In Molev, Gelfand-Tsetlin patterns with negative entries and rows in decreasing order was used. However, we will not be following their convention.

Matrix Element Formulas

The Lie algebra \(\mathfrak{so}(2n)\) and \(\mathfrak{so}(2n+1)\) are \(J_{2p, 2p+1}\) and \(J_{2p+1, 2p+2}\), where \(J_{i,j} = E_{i,j} - E_{j,i}, i < j\).

According to

  • Gelfand, I. M., and M. L. Zetlin. Finite-dimensional representations of the orthogonal group. Math. USSR Doklady. Vol. 71. 1950,

the generators \(J_{2p, 2p+1}\) and \(J_{2p+1, 2p+2}\) act on the Gelfand-Tsetlin basis \(\xi_\Lambda\) of the unitary representation by: \[ \begin{array}{c} J_{2p+1,2p}\xi_\Lambda=\sum_{j=1}^{p}A_{2p-1,j}^+\xi_{\Lambda_{2p-1,j}^+}-\sum_{j=1}^{p}A_{2p-1,j}^-\xi_{\Lambda_{2p-1,j}^-}\\ J_{2p+2,2p+1}\xi_\Lambda=\sum_{j=1}^{p}B_{2p,j}^+\xi_{\Lambda_{2p,j}^+}-\sum_{j=1}^{p}B_{2p,j}^-\xi_{\Lambda_{2p,j}^-}+iC_{2p}\xi_\Lambda \end{array} \] with the coefficients given by \[ A_{2p-1,j}^+=\left|\frac{\prod_{r=1}^{p-1}\left(l_{2p-2,r}-l_{2p-1,j}-1\right)\left(l_{2p-2,r}+l_{2p-1,j}\right)\prod_{r=1}^{p}\left(l_{2p,r}-l_{2p-1,j}-1\right)\left(l_{2p,r}+l_{2p-1,j}\right)}{\prod_{r\neq j}\left(l_{2p-1,r}^{2}-l_{2p-1,j}^{2}\right)\left(l_{2p-1,r}^{2}-\left(l_{2p-1,j}+1\right)^{2}\right)}\right|^{1/2} \] \[ A_{2p-1,j}^-=\left|\frac{\prod_{r=1}^{p-1}\left(l_{2p-2,r}-l_{2p-1,j}\right)\left(l_{2p-2,r}+l_{2p-1,j}-1\right)\prod_{r=1}^{p}\left(l_{2p,r}-l_{2p-1,j}\right)\left(l_{2p,r}+l_{2p-1,j}-1\right)}{\prod_{r\neq j}\left(l_{2p-1,r}^{2}-(l_{2p-1,j}-1)^{2}\right)\left(l_{2p-1,r}^{2}-l_{2p-1,j}^{2}\right)}\right|^{1/2} \] \[ B_{2p,j}^+=\left|\frac{\prod_{r=1}^{p}\left(l_{2p-1,r}^{2}-l_{2p,j}^{2}\right)\prod_{r=1}^{p+1}\left(l_{2p+1,r}^{2}-l_{2p,j}^{2}\right)}{l_{2p,j}^{2}\left(4l_{2p,j}^{2}-1\right)\prod_{r\neq j}\left(l_{2p,r}^{2}-l_{2p,j}^{2}\right)\left(\left(l_{2p,r}-1\right)^{2}-l_{2p,j}^{2}\right)}\right|^{1/2} \] \[ B_{2p,j}^-=\left|\frac{\prod_{r=1}^{p}\left(l_{2p-1,r}^{2}-\left(l_{2p,j}-1\right)^{2}\right)\prod_{r=1}^{p+1}\left(l_{2p+1,r}^{2}-\left(l_{2p,j}-1\right)^{2}\right)}{\left(l_{2p,j}-1\right)^{2}\left(4\left(l_{2p,j}-1\right)^{2}-1\right)\prod_{r\neq j}\left(l_{2p,r}^{2}-\left(l_{2p,j}-1\right)^{2}\right)\left(\left(l_{2p,r}-1\right)^{2}-\left(l_{2p,j}-1\right)^{2}\right)}\right|^{1/2} \] \[ C_{2 p}=\frac{\prod_{r=1}^{p} l_{2 p-1, r} \prod_{r=1}^{p+1} l_{2 p+1, r}}{\prod_{r=1}^{p} l_{2 p, r}\left(l_{2 p, r}-1\right)} \]

The actions of the generators on the basis elements \(\zeta_{\Lambda}\) with rational coefficients are given by: \[ J_{2p, 2p+1} \zeta_{\Lambda}= \sum_{j=1}^p a_{2p-1,j}^+\zeta_{\Lambda_{2p-1,j}^+} - \sum_{j=1}^p a_{2p-1,j}^-\zeta_{\Lambda_{2p-1,j}^-} \] \[ J_{2p+1, 2p+2} \xi_{\Lambda}= \sum_{j=1}^p b_{2p,j}^+\zeta_{\Lambda_{2p,j}^+} - \sum_{j=1}^p b_{2p,j}^-\zeta_{\Lambda_{2p,j}^-} + \sum_{j=1}^p i c_\Lambda\zeta_{\Lambda} \] with coefficients \[ a_{2p-1,j}^+ = \left|\frac{\prod_{r=1}^{p-1}\left(l_{2p-2,r}+l_{2p-1,j}\right)\left(l_{2p-2,r}-l_{2p-1,j}-1\right)\prod_{r=1}^{p}\left(l_{2p,r} + l_{2p-1,j}\right)\left(l_{2p,r}-l_{2p-1,j}-1\right)}{\prod_{r=1,r\neq j}^{p}\left(l_{2p-1,r}+l_{2p-1,j}\right)\left(l_{2p-1,r}-l_{2p-1,j}\right)}\right| \]

\[ a_{2p-1,j}^- = \left|\frac{1}{\prod_{r=1,r\neq j}^{p}\left(l_{2p-1,r}+l_{2p-1,j}\right)\left(l_{2p-1,r}-l_{2p-1,j}\right)}\right| \] \[ b_{\Lambda_{2p,j}^+} = \left|\frac{\prod_{r=1}^p \left(l_{2p-1,r}+l_{2p,j}\right)\prod_{r=1}^{p+1}\left(l_{2p+1,r} + l_{2p,j}\right)}{2 l_{2p,j}^2 \left(4 l_{2p,j}^2 - 1\right)\prod_{r=1,r\neq j}^p \left(l_{2p,r} - l_{2p,j}\right)\left(l_{2p,r} + l_{2p,j}-1\right)}\right| \]

\[ b_{\Lambda_{2p,j}^-} = \left|\frac{\prod_{r=1}^p \left(l_{2p-1,r}-l_{2p,j}+1\right)\prod_{r=1}^{p+1}\left(l_{2p+1,r} - l_{2p,j}+1\right)}{\prod_{r=1,r\neq j}^p \left(l_{2p,r} - l_{2p,j}\right)\left(l_{2p,r} + l_{2p,j} -1\right)}\right| \] \[ c_\Lambda = \frac{\prod_{r=1}^{p}l_{2p-1,r}\prod_{r=1}^{p+1} l_{2p+1,r}}{\prod_{r=1}^{p} l_{2p,r}\left(l_{2p,r}-1\right)} \]
